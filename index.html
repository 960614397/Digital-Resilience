POPIA Changes Everything for South African Businesses

The Protection of Personal Information Act carries fines of up to R10 million and criminal liability for responsible parties. A breach is no longer just an IT problem — it is a board-level legal and financial exposure. Most organisations are still not adequately prepared.

What Happens When You're Not Ready

The cost of a breach far exceeds the cost of prevention. Here is what organisations that delay action typically face.

Operational Shutdown

Business Disruption

Ransomware can take an organisation offline for days or weeks. Every hour of downtime carries a direct revenue cost — plus recovery, forensics, and remediation expenses on top.

POPIA, PCI-DSS, and sector-specific regulations carry significant financial penalties for inadequate data protection. Regulators are increasingly active in enforcement across South Africa.

"We Had a Near-Miss. We Can't Be This Exposed Again."

The Challenge

An organisation relying on legacy AV with no 24/7 monitoring experienced a near-miss ransomware event. Leadership demanded a complete rethink of detection and response — fast.

Our Approach

Deployed ZeroDwell Containment at the endpoint — isolating unknown processes before execution — combined with our 24/7 SOC for real-time investigation and response.

OutcomeZero successful ransomware executions post-deployment. Mean time to detect reduced from days to under 8 minutes.
🏢

"We Have No Visibility Into Our Suppliers' Security."

The Challenge

Hundreds of technology vendors with no systematic way to assess their cyber posture — creating significant regulatory exposure and supply chain risk.

Our Approach

Deployed Third-Party Cyber Risk Intelligence with continuous vendor rating, Ransomware Susceptibility Indexing, and automated alerts on supplier posture changes.

OutcomeFull vendor risk visibility within 30 days. High-risk suppliers identified and remediation mandated before any incident occurred.

"Our Audit Is Coming and We're Not Ready."

OutcomeFull compliance achieved within 6 months. Audit preparation time reduced by 70%.

"We've Been Breached Before. The Board Now Wants Insurance — But Our Broker Says Our Risk Profile Is Too High."

The Challenge

After a significant incident, an organisation's cyber insurance premiums skyrocketed and their broker flagged gaps in their security controls that were putting full coverage at risk. Leadership needed both coverage and a credible path to improving their risk posture.

Our Approach

We engaged eInsurer — DSG's cyber insurance division — to conduct a full risk assessment and develop a tailored ProBusiness Protect policy matched to the organisation's actual risk profile. OutcomeFull cyber insurance coverage secured within 6 weeks. Ongoing premium reductions tied to security milestones — turning insurance from a cost into a programme that actively funds better security.

"We Hold Digital Assets and Have No Recovery Plan If Our Wallet Infrastructure Is Compromised."

The Challenge

A financial services organisation holding significant digital assets on behalf of clients had no automated response to wallet compromise or infrastructure failure. Their recovery process was entirely manual — in a scenario where seconds matter, this was an unacceptable risk to client funds and regulatory standing.

Our Approach

Deployed Circuit Security's Automatic Asset Extraction (AAE) technology across their wallet infrastructure — instantly sweeping assets to pre-approved secure vaults upon any compromise event, with zero key escrow. Integrated with their existing MPC wallet stack and layered into the Digital Resilience SOC for real-time incident visibility and response coordination.

OutcomeAutomated asset protection in place within days of deployment. Cyber insurance premiums reduced by 12% following insurer validation of the AAE technology. First organisation in their peer group with a fully documented digital asset incident response plan.

"We're Losing Revenue to Fraud We Can't See — Account Takeovers, Fake Signups, and Bot Traffic Are Killing Our Metrics."

The Challenge

A digital commerce and payments organisation was suffering escalating account takeover fraud, bot-driven inventory abuse, and synthetic identity signups that were undermining revenue, skewing analytics, and creating chargeback liability — all while traditional fraud tools were generating too many false positives to be operationally viable.

Our Approach

Deployed Innerworks across the client's digital environment — applying device fingerprinting, behavioural analytics, and location intelligence to distinguish real users from compromised accounts, bots, and synthetic identities in real time. The RedTeam simulation programme was run first to map exactly which attack vectors were being exploited, ensuring the deployment was tuned to the client's specific threat profile from day one.

OutcomeAccount takeover incidents reduced by 74% within 60 days. Bot traffic to the platform dropped by over 80%. Chargeback rate fell significantly, recovering meaningful revenue previously lost to fraud.

Xcitium
Zero Trust · ZeroDwell · XDR · CNAPP
Problem Solved
Traditional security tools rely on knowing what a threat looks like before blocking it — leaving organisations exposed to unknown malware, zero-days, and novel ransomware variants.
Xcitium's patented ZeroDwell Containment technology takes a fundamentally different approach: every unknown process is automatically containerised at kernel level and run in isolation until it is proven safe — so threats can never execute, even if they are never identified. This eliminates the detection gap that plagues signature- and behaviour-based tools. Combined with XDR and cloud-native application protection (CNAPP), Xcitium delivers Zero Trust enforcement from endpoint to cloud without sacrificing user productivity.
